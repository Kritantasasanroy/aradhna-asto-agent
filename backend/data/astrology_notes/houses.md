# The Houses

Each house rules a different area of life. The sign on the cusp of the house and any planets inside it color how that life area plays out for this person.

## 1st House — Self, Identity, Physical Appearance
The house of "I am." It's how you come across to the world, your instinctive self-presentation, and your physical body. The sign rising on the 1st house cusp is the Ascendant — one of the most important points in the chart. Planets in the 1st house are prominently expressed in the personality and are often physically visible.

## 2nd House — Money, Possessions, Values
What you earn, what you own, and what you value most deeply. The 2nd house describes your relationship with material resources and your sense of self-worth. Planets here can show talents you use to make money, or issues around financial security.

## 3rd House — Communication, Siblings, Local Travel, Learning
How your mind works in everyday life. Your communication style, your relationship with siblings and neighbors, how you learn, and short trips and commutes. Mercury in the 3rd is at home here.

## 4th House — Home, Family, Roots, the Past
The foundation of the chart. Your relationship with your family of origin, your sense of home, your ancestry, and your private inner life. It also rules where you end up — your home in the second half of life. The IC (Imum Coeli) is the cusp of this house.

## 5th House — Creativity, Romance, Children, Pleasure
Joy, play, creative self-expression, love affairs, and children. This house rules what you do for fun and what makes your heart sing. Leo naturally rules this house.

## 6th House — Work, Health, Daily Routines, Service
Day-to-day work (not career, which is the 10th), health habits, service to others, and the small routines that make up a life. Planets here often describe working style and relationship to the body.

## 7th House — Partnerships, Marriage, Open Enemies
The house of one-on-one relationships — marriage, business partnerships, and significant others. The Descendant is the cusp of the 7th. Planets here describe what we seek in partners and sometimes what we project outward.

## 8th House — Death, Transformation, Shared Resources, the Occult
The deep house. It rules shared finances (debt, taxes, inheritance), psychological transformation, sexuality as a transformative act, and matters of death and rebirth. Pluto and Scorpio naturally resonate here.

## 9th House — Philosophy, Higher Education, Travel, Spirituality, Law
Long-distance travel, higher learning, belief systems, religion, law, and the search for meaning. Where you go to expand your mind and find your philosophy. Jupiter naturally rules here.

## 10th House — Career, Public Image, Authority, Legacy
What you're known for in the world. Your career path, public reputation, relationship with authority, and the legacy you leave. The Midheaven (MC) is the cusp. Planets here are often prominent in public life.

## 11th House — Friends, Community, Hopes, Social Groups
Your tribe — the communities you belong to, your friendships, your social vision, and your hopes and wishes. Uranus and Aquarius naturally resonate here.

## 12th House — Solitude, Spirituality, Hidden Matters, the Unconscious
The house of what's hidden — from others and from yourself. It rules retreat, spiritual practice, self-undoing, unconscious patterns, and what's behind the scenes. Planets in the 12th work in subtle, often private ways and can be difficult to access consciously.
