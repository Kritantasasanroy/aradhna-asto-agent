# Reading Transits

## What transits are
A transit is simply a planet in the sky right now making an angular relationship (an aspect) to a planet in your natal chart. Your natal chart is fixed — it's a snapshot of the sky the moment you were born. Transiting planets move through it over your lifetime, activating different areas.

## How to read a transit
Look at the transiting planet (what's it about?), the aspect (is it flowing or tense?), and the natal planet (which part of the person's life and psyche is being touched?). Then layer in the houses involved for more specificity.

For example: transiting Jupiter trine natal Venus. Jupiter = expansion and abundance. Trine = flowing, easy. Venus = love, money, values. This transit often brings a warm, pleasant period in relationships or finances — opportunities arise without much friction.

## Planet timing — how long transits last
- Sun: 2-3 days per aspect
- Moon: hours — felt emotionally, passes quickly
- Mercury: days
- Venus: days to weeks
- Mars: 1-2 weeks
- Jupiter: weeks to a few months
- Saturn: weeks to months (especially significant)
- Uranus, Neptune, Pluto: months to years (the slow ones reshape things deeply)

## Most significant transits
The outer planets — Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto — create the major life chapters when they aspect natal planets or angles (Ascendant, MC). The faster planets (Sun, Moon, Mars) trigger those areas in the moment.

Saturn transiting over the natal Ascendant can bring a year of feeling restricted, more serious, more visible in a demanding way. Saturn over the Midheaven often coincides with major career shifts. Uranus over the Ascendant can bring sudden life changes, a reinvented identity, or unexpected events.

## Transits to natal Sun
The Sun is your core identity and vitality. Transits to the natal Sun often mark significant identity-related periods.
- Jupiter conjunct Sun: expansion, confidence, opportunities, good fortune
- Saturn conjunct Sun: consolidation, responsibility, feeling the weight of things
- Uranus conjunct Sun: sudden change, reinvention, disruption to identity
- Neptune conjunct Sun: confusion about identity, spiritual awakening, idealism
- Pluto conjunct Sun: powerful transformation, confronting power dynamics

## Transits to natal Moon
Moon transits touch the emotional body and personal life — home, family, inner world.
- Jupiter trine Moon: emotional warmth, family happiness, good period for home and nurturing
- Saturn square Moon: emotional restriction, difficult family dynamics, needing to build emotional resilience
- Pluto conjunct Moon: deep emotional transformation, sometimes loss

## Transits to natal Venus
Venus transits affect relationships, money, and pleasure.
- Jupiter conjunct Venus: one of the luckiest transits for love and money
- Saturn square Venus: testing of relationships, sometimes endings or difficult emotional work
- Uranus conjunct Venus: sudden changes in relationships — sometimes exciting, sometimes disruptive

## Transits to natal Mars
Mars transits energize and sometimes agitate whatever they touch.
- Jupiter conjunct Mars: big energy, ambition, taking action on big goals
- Saturn conjunct Mars: frustrating — energy is blocked or delayed, lessons in patience and strategy
- Pluto conjunct Mars: intense, powerful drive — can be transformative if channeled well

## The current sky
Always look at both what's transiting and what's in the natal chart. A transiting Jupiter through the 10th house is generally a positive career period, but how positive depends on what natal planets are there and how well Jupiter aspects the rest of the chart.
