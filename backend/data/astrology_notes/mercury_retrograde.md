# Mercury Retrograde

## What it is
Mercury goes retrograde three to four times a year for about three weeks each time. "Retrograde" means the planet appears to move backward in the sky from our perspective on Earth — it's an optical illusion based on the relative speeds of Earth and Mercury in their orbits.

## What it actually affects
Mercury rules communication, technology, contracts, transportation, and the thinking mind. When Mercury is retrograde, these areas can feel sticky, prone to misunderstandings, or delayed. It's a period when messages get lost, machines act up, travel plans run into snags, and conversations get muddled.

The advice to "not sign contracts during Mercury retrograde" comes from this — the details are more likely to be missed or misunderstood. It's not that things signed will inevitably fail, but you may want to read everything twice.

## Who feels it most
People with Mercury prominent in their natal chart — Mercury in Gemini, Mercury in Virgo, Mercury conjunct the Sun or Ascendant, or Mercury in the 1st or 3rd house — tend to feel Mercury retrogrades more acutely. Also, the retrograde affects the signs it's transiting more directly.

## Natal Mercury retrograde
If someone is born with Mercury retrograde (Mercury was retrograde at their birth), they often have an interior, reflective thinking style. They process information deeply and may think before speaking rather than thinking out loud. They're not necessarily worse communicators — often they're more careful and considered.

## What to do
Mercury retrograde is a natural period of reflection and review. The "re" words fit: revisit, reconsider, revise, redo. Old contacts and old ideas resurface. It's a good time to go back to something unfinished, reconnect with someone you've lost touch with, or review a project before launching.

When Mercury stations direct (comes out of retrograde), there's often a clearing feeling — communication gets unstuck, decisions feel easier to make.
