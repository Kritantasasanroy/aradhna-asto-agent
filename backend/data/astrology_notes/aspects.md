# Aspects

Aspects are the angles between planets in the chart. They describe how two planetary energies relate to each other — whether they flow easily, create tension, or blend in complex ways.

## Conjunction (0°, orb 8°)
Two planets sitting close together in the same area of sky. Their energies merge — they become hard to separate. Conjunctions intensify both planets. Whether this is easy or difficult depends on which planets are involved. Sun conjunct Jupiter is expansive and fortunate. Sun conjunct Saturn can feel heavy and restricted.

## Sextile (60°, orb 4°)
A supportive, flowing aspect. The two planets work together easily and create opportunities when you act on them. Sextiles are gentle — they don't force growth but reward effort. Less powerful than a trine but more productive because there's a slight edge of effort required.

## Square (90°, orb 6°)
The classic tension aspect. Two planets pulling in incompatible directions, creating friction, challenges, and the need to work something out. Squares are where we grow the most, even if they're uncomfortable. They don't let us rest. A Saturn square Mercury person might have to work harder at communication than most — but can develop remarkable mental discipline.

## Trine (120°, orb 6°)
Natural, easy flow between two planets. Their energies support each other without effort. Trines show natural talents and gifts. The shadow side is that they can make us complacent — we take the gift for granted. A Venus trine Jupiter person has natural charm and warmth; they may never have had to consciously develop social skills because it came so easily.

## Opposition (180°, orb 8°)
Two planets facing each other across the chart — they pull in opposite directions. Like a tug of war. The challenge is integration: learning to hold both energies rather than oscillating between them or projecting one onto other people. Sun opposite Moon (born at a full moon) creates an inner tension between ego and emotional needs.

## Quincunx / Inconjunct (150°, orb 3°)
An awkward aspect where two planets have nothing in common — different element, different modality, different quality. They require constant adjustment and never quite resolve. Often shows up as blind spots or areas of life that need repeated recalibration.

## Transit Aspects
When we talk about transits, we're looking at these same aspects but between a planet in the sky right now and a planet in the natal chart. A transiting Saturn square natal Sun is a significant period of restriction and testing. A transiting Jupiter trine natal Venus can bring a welcome period of ease in relationships or finances.
