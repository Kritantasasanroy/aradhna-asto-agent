# The Planets

## The Sun
The Sun is the core of the chart — identity, ego, purpose, and the energy you radiate into the world. It describes who you are becoming, not just who you were born as. The Sun sign shows how you want to shine and be seen. A strong, well-aspected Sun means confidence and vitality. A challenged Sun (by hard aspects from Saturn or Pluto, for example) can mean someone who had to fight hard to develop their sense of self. The house placement shows where in life you most want to express yourself.

## The Moon
The Moon is your emotional body — instincts, subconscious patterns, what you need to feel safe and nurtured. It's the part of you that reacts before you think. The Moon sign shows how you process feelings and what you need emotionally. Moon in water signs (Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces) tends toward deep emotional sensitivity. Moon in earth signs (Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn) needs physical comfort and stability. Moon in air signs (Gemini, Libra, Aquarius) processes feelings through thought. Moon in fire signs (Aries, Leo, Sagittarius) can feel emotions intensely but moves through them quickly.

## Mercury
Mercury governs how you think, communicate, and learn. It rules your voice, your writing, your day-to-day mind. Mercury's sign shows your communication style — Mercury in Gemini talks fast and loves ideas, Mercury in Taurus thinks slowly but builds solid understanding. Mercury retrograde periods (three to four times a year) often bring communication delays and misunderstandings, and can be felt most strongly by people with natal Mercury in a prominent position.

## Venus
Venus rules love, beauty, pleasure, and values. It describes what you find attractive, how you behave in relationships, and your relationship with money and possessions. Venus in Taurus loves sensory pleasure and loyalty. Venus in Scorpio craves intensity and depth. Venus in Aquarius values freedom and friendship within love. The house placement shows where you look for love and beauty in your life.

## Mars
Mars is drive, desire, courage, and anger. It shows how you go after what you want and how you handle conflict. Mars in Aries is direct and impulsive. Mars in Capricorn is disciplined and strategic. A well-aspected Mars means healthy assertion and physical vitality. Mars in hard aspect to Saturn can create frustration or a sense of blocked energy. Mars transits often bring action, urgency, or friction to the house they're moving through.

## Jupiter
Jupiter expands and protects what it touches. It represents abundance, optimism, higher learning, travel, and wisdom. Jupiter's house placement shows where life tends to be generous with you and where you naturally grow. Jupiter transits (spending about a year in each sign) bring opportunities and expansion to the themes of the house they're transiting. Jupiter square or opposition can sometimes indicate overconfidence or excess.

## Saturn
Saturn is the teacher. It represents discipline, structure, responsibility, karma, and the places in life where we have to work for what we get. Saturn's house placement shows where we face our greatest challenges and — over time — our greatest mastery. Saturn return (around ages 28-30, 58-60) is a major life reckoning where the structures you've built get tested and rebuilt. Saturn transits (spending about 2.5 years per sign) bring seriousness, focus, and lessons to whatever they touch.

## Uranus
Uranus rules sudden change, rebellion, innovation, and the breaking of old patterns. Where Uranus sits in the natal chart shows where you need freedom and resist convention. Uranus transits are often disruptive but ultimately liberating — they clear out what's no longer working. Uranus square Uranus (around age 21 and 63) and Uranus opposition Uranus (around age 42) are generational milestone transits tied to personal reinvention.

## Neptune
Neptune dissolves boundaries and rules dreams, spirituality, illusion, and compassion. Where Neptune falls in the chart shows where we seek transcendence but might also deceive ourselves. Neptune transits can bring inspiration and spiritual opening, but also confusion and idealization. Neptune conjunct personal planets can blur the edges of identity or relationships in that area of life.

## Pluto
Pluto rules transformation, power, death and rebirth, and the deep unconscious. Where Pluto sits in the natal chart shows where we carry potential for profound transformation — and also for obsession or compulsion. Pluto transits are long (several years per sign), slow-moving, and often involve complete dismantling and rebuilding of whatever they touch. Pluto crossing the Ascendant, Midheaven, or a personal planet is considered one of the most significant transits in a person's life.
